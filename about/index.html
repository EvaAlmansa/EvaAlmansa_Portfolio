About

I am a Marie Skłodowska-Curie Early Stage Researcher in the Visual Computing (ViC) group at the Center for Advanced Studies, Research, and Development in Sardinia (CRS4). I hold a Bachelor Degree in Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence (2015) and a Master Degree of Data Science (2017) from the University of Granada, Spain. I am awarded a fellowship (2020-2023) from the EVOCATION innovative training network under H2020.

  • Eva Almansa | Semantic Scholar
  • My work’s summary at CRS4 Visual Computing
  • Supervisors: Enrico Gobbetti and Giovanni Pintore (CRS4)
  • Techniques: Deep Learning applied from a single panoramic image to estimate depth maps, estimate 3D mesh model of the boundary surfaces of the architectural layout, RGB-D empty room estimation, and sparse to dense depth estimation.

2020-Currently (until December 2023)

  • Currently, I am working as an Early Stage Researcher (ESR) and a Marie Skłodowska-Curie Fellow (2020-2023) in the Advanced Visual and Geometric Computing for 3D Capture, Display, and Fabrication (EVOCATION) project. Within EVOCATION, I am hosted by Center for Advanced Studies, Research and Development in Sardinia (CRS4), a beneficiary of this project, and doing my PhD work at the University of Cagliari (UniCa), associate partner of the project. My research supervisor within EVOCATION and at CRS4 are Dr. Enrico Gobbetti, director of Visual and Data-intensive Computing at CRS4, and Dr. Giovanni Pintore, senior research engineer in the same group, while my academic supervisor is Prof. Riccardo Scateni, professor of Computer Science at UniCa.
